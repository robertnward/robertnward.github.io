Robert Ward

I'm a PhD student in Public Policy and MS student in Computer Science at Georgia Tech, advised by Cassidy R. Sugimoto and John P. Walsh. I'm also a visiting student researcher with Marc Santolini in the Interaction Data Lab at LPI (Université Paris Cité) and at Stanford University with Megan Palmer.

Before that, I earned a BS in Molecular Genetics and an MPA from The Ohio State University where I worked briefly at the Battelle Center for Science, Engineering and Public Policy.

My work has been supported by the Georgia Tech President's Fellowship and grants from the National Science Foundation, French National Research Agency, Google and University of Vermont Complex Systems Center.

Research

I am an organizational theorist using computational methods to uncover the social roots of innovation. On the production side, I'm interested in how social interactions affect organizational structure and innovation. On the consumption side, I'm interested in how social expectations affect the reception of people and ideas.
