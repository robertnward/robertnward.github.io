I am a Ph.D. candidate in Public Policy at Georgia Institute of Technology, a visiting student researcher at Stanford University and in the Interaction Data Lab (Learning Planet Institute, Universit√© Paris Cit√©).

How do people and organizations form relationships? How do they combine and connect old ideas to create new innovations?

In my research, I use computational methods to understand the social and cognitive organization of scientific and creative work. My current projects are focused on two streams. In the context of social networks, I am interested in understanding when and why self-organization fails, and in designing interventions on network formation that improve creative outcomes, and welfare in general. In my other line of work, I study how categories shape the evaluation of atypical contributors and contributions to scientific knowledge.

I will be joining the Knowledge Lab at University of Chicago as a postdoc later this year.
