Research

Areas: computational social science; organization theory; networks; machine learning; science, technology and innovation

My research develops and applies computational methods to understand the organizational roots of innovation in science and technology. On the production side, I am interested in how social interactions affect organizational structure and innovation. On the consumption side, I am interested in how the content of scientific texts affects the success of authors and ideas. At the macro-level, I am interested in how the interaction between these production and consumption dynamics affects the evolution of scientific communities and knowledge.
