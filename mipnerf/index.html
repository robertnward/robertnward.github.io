The organization of science is changing. In professional settings, the production of new knowledge is increasingly performed by teams, of increasing size, complex structure and patterns of coordination. Science is shifting too outside its professional context into citizen and crowd settings in which amateurs freely enter and work alone or in collaboration with industry and academic partners. What do these shifts mean for the landscape of scientific knowledge? for the life and death of organizations? For the experience and careers of their members? How can we understand and harness new organizational forms to produce innovations in science and technology? The goal of this workshop is to bring together a small and selected group of scholars to present and discuss research, and spur follow on collaboration around these questions.
